Welcome to Under the Sea!     

There are three main layers under the sea.

  1. The Sunlit Zone, between 0-200m, is the one where us humans swim, containing fish, dolphins, sharks, etc!
  2. Beneath that is the Twilight Zone. With its depth of between 200-1000m, much less sunlight reaches this region. Very little plant life grows here (plants need sunlight to grow!).
  3. The Dark Zone (a.k.a. the Midnight Zone) is the darkest zone out of all three. Reaching up to 4000m in depth, no sunlight can reach this region. All the animals living here have adapted to the darkness, and don't need light in order to be able to survive.

The last two regions are the deepest parts of the ocean, the abyss and the trenches. Animals inhabit these zones, but they too are adapted to the darkness and water pressure of this depth.
