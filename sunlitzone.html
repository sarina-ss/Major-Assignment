The Sunlit Zone
         

Many animals live in the sunlit zone. These include sharks, turtles, seals, tuna, jellyfish and more! Numerous plant species also live in the sunlit zone, as the amount of sunlight provided allows them to photosynthesize. Some species of animals in the sunlit zone have a feature called countershading, where they are light at the bottom and dark at the top. This allows them to be virtually invisible to predators. If they are looked at by predators from above them, their dark skin will blend in with the deep sea. If they are looked at by predators beneath them, they will blend in with the light provided by the sun.

Due to how close all of these animals are to the surface, they are extremely accessible to humans. This makes them a common source of food and sport, which can be ethically controversial. To participate in a research study and voice your thoughts about this matter, email: sunlightzone@fishing.ca
